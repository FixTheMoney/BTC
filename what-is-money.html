What is Money?

Money is one of humanity's most important inventions. It serves as a medium of exchange, a unit of account, and a store of value. Understanding money is essential to understanding economics, society, and the financial system.

The Three Functions of Money

  • Medium of Exchange: Money allows us to trade goods and services without the need for direct barter. Instead of trading apples for oranges, we can sell apples for money and buy oranges with that money.
  • Unit of Account: Money provides a common measure of value. Prices expressed in money allow us to compare the value of different goods and services.
  • Store of Value: Money allows us to save purchasing power for the future. We can earn money today and spend it tomorrow, next month, or next year.
